King David

King, Warrior (1035 - 970 BCE)

10 Things You Should Know About David

  • David was a shepherd from a little boy till the age of 18. He tended the flock of his father as the last born of 8 sons and some daughters. He spent most of his life in the woods and in the forest, which gave him the opportunity to observe God's creation and develop deep appreciation and trust in God as a little boy. Those qualities were what helped him through his trialsome times.
  • He single-handedly killed a lion and a bear as a little boy under the age of 12. The wild beasts' had each come to carry off a sheep from his father's flock. But David went out after them with his sling, stone and staff. He rescued the sheep from their mouth and killed the beasts in the end showing his courage and trust in God even as a little boy.
  • When God's spirit left King Saul and Israel needed another King, David was God's next choice of King and he was anointed as the next king by Prophet Samuel at the age of 12. Even though he had brothers who were older and stronger than him, he was still the one God choose to be the next king.
  • After David was anointed, a bad spirit was terrorizing King Saul. He needed something to calm his nerves as he was always in bad mood. David was recommended as a skilled harpist to play soothing music to King Saul. He probably must have developed the skill during his years in the field with the sheep, making King Saul to love David because his music made him feel better.
  • He killed a fearsome giant named Goliath who was over 9 feet tall. Goliath taunted the Israelites to bring a man able to fight him. David who was at the age of 18 at the time agreed to fight the giant. He went after him with just a sling and stones, putting his complete trust in God to help him fight the giant. He eventually killed the giant with a stone in the forehead.
  • David was a mighty warrior in Israel, which made Saul to put him in charge of the men of war. He was loved and respected by everyone, proving himself a better soldier than Saul. The women even composed a song for him making the king of Israel jealous of him.
  • He was a songwriter who wrote over a 100 songs, most of which are written in the bible in the book of psalms. His songs were songs of praises to God who protected him throughout all his life. The songs were written during or after most events in his life, even when his best friend Joenathan, Saul son, died alongside Saul in a battle with the philistines.
  • He became king at the age of 30, after Saul pursued David out of jealousy with the intentions of killing him. Meanwhile, Joenathan was a backbone for David always showing him support and love throughout till his death alongside his father Saul. Which made David as the anointed one to become the next king of Israel.
  • He married about 8 wives by the names of: Michal, Ahinoam, Abigail, Maachah, Haggith, Abital, Eglah, Bathesheba. This women bore him more than 18 children. He was succeeded by his son Solomon.
  • David was a good king, but he made serious mistakes. He watched Bethsheba another mans wife bathing. He desired her and slept with her, he then killed the woman's husband to cover his act. This act brought calamity to his household, but he was later forgiven by God. Overall David prove to be a good king of Israel. His rule brought peace and prosperity to the land of Israel.
